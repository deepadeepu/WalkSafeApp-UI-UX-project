Walking Safety UX Case Study

Using secondary research to design safer urban walking experiences

Overview

This project explores how urban walking safety can be improved through thoughtful UX design. Using secondary research, the goal was to identify risks, user behaviors, and opportunities for intervention.

Problem

Pedestrians often feel unsafe due to poor lighting, traffic risks, and lack of real-time safety information. Existing navigation apps prioritize speed over safety.

Research Insights

Lighting Matters

Poorly lit streets significantly increase perceived danger, especially at night.

Gender Differences

Women report higher safety concerns and prefer well-populated routes.

Route Awareness

Users lack access to real-time safety data when choosing routes.

User Needs

  • Safe route recommendations
  • Real-time alerts (crime, hazards)
  • Visibility of lighting and foot traffic
  • Emergency assistance features

Solution

A mobile app that integrates safety data into navigation by prioritizing well-lit, populated, and lower-risk routes.

Key Features

Safe Routing

Routes optimized based on safety metrics instead of just speed.

Live Alerts

Real-time notifications for nearby incidents.

Community Reports

Users contribute safety insights and updates.

Emergency Mode

Quick access to contacts and authorities.

Design Process

Research → Synthesis → Ideation → Prototyping → Testing

Impact

This solution empowers users to make informed decisions, increasing confidence and reducing perceived risk when walking in urban environments.
